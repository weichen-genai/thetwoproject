Research

Research mapped to T–W–O

Formal work on generative AI becomes easier to read when it is tied back to Tasks, Workflows, and Organizations.

This page uses a visual T–W–O structure to organize research. Within each layer, we distinguish between work from the Workflow Lab and other people’s papers that illuminate similar questions.

Source

Workflow Lab research and external work.

View the full research page

T

Task

Individual units of work where GenAI changes execution quality, speed, or cost.

W

Workflow

Sequences of tasks, coordination, and operating routines that determine how work actually moves.

O

Organization

Structure, governance, and strategy that shape where GenAI fits and who owns change.

Task

How GenAI changes discrete tasks.

Work in this layer focuses on the behavior of models and humans at the level of individual tasks or prompts.

Other research

Representative experiments on AI-assisted writing and coding

Maps to Task because these papers study how GenAI support changes the speed and quality of specific professional tasks such as drafting, editing, and programming.

Examples include field and lab experiments on GenAI-assisted writing and software development.

Workflow

How GenAI reshapes sequences of work.

Work in this layer is about end-to-end processes, not just single prompts.

Other research

Field studies of GenAI in real service and knowledge-work flows

Maps to Workflow because these studies look at how GenAI alters handoffs, monitoring, and coordination across multiple steps of a process.

Examples include experiments embedding GenAI in customer support, education, and professional services workflows.

Organization

GenAI at the level of structure and governance.

Work in this layer is about who owns AI, how it is governed, and how it reshapes organizational design.

Other research

Broader work on AI, productivity, and organizational design

Maps to Organization because it studies how AI adoption interacts with firm boundaries, management practices, and labor markets.

This includes empirical and theoretical papers on the organizational and macro implications of AI.
