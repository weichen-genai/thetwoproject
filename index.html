Task, Workflow, Organization

The T-W-O Project

AI changes tasks. Transformation depends on workflow redesign and organizational ownership.

T-W-O is a simple way to think about where AI creates leverage, where it creates friction, and what has to change for gains to compound.

The framework

T-W-O looks at AI change across three connected layers.

At the task level, AI shifts how work gets done. At the workflow level, teams must redesign how tasks connect, exceptions are handled, and decisions move. At the organization level, leaders have to assign ownership, incentives, and governance so the redesign can hold.

The missing middle

Most AI strategies jump between tools and enterprise ambition without redesigning the workflow in between.

That is why early wins often stay local. Task-level gains are real, but they do not become durable operating advantages unless someone changes the workflow that connects people, systems, and judgments.

Why workflow matters

Workflow is where validation, escalation, sequencing, and accountability live. If that layer stays untouched, AI adds effort as often as it removes effort.
